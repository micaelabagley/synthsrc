class synthsrc.attenuation.IGMAttenuation¶

Bases: object

Class to calculate and apply attenuation from the intergalactic medium.

inoue(wave, zz)¶

Calculates IGM attenuation following Inoue et al. (2014).

The attenuation by the intergalactic medium is taken from the analytic model of Inoue et al. (2014), which is preferred over Madau (1995) because they inlcude more Lyman series lines, the contribution from damped Lyman-alpha systems, and a better treatment of the Lyman continuum.

inoue() requires inoue_coeffs.dat, which can be found in synthsrc/requiredfiles.

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Array of wavelength values
  • zz (float) – Redshift of source, z NOT (1+z)
Returns:

ext – The absorption array, \(e^{-\tau^{IGM}_{\lambda_{obs}}}\)
Return type:

float
class synthsrc.attenuation.ISMExtinction¶

Bases: object

Class to calculate and apply extinction from the interstellar medium.

Options are:

  • calz() - Calzetti et al. (2000)
  • ccm() - `Cardelli, Clayton, and Mathis (1989)
  • allen() - Allen (1976)
  • prevot() - Prevot et al. (1984)
  • seaton() - Seaton (1979)
  • fitz() - Fitzpatrick (1986)

For all reddening laws, supplying a negative ebv will deredden fluxes.

allen(wave, flux, ebv, RV=3.1)¶

Applies the reddening law from Allen (1976).

The reddening law:

\[\frac{A_{\lambda}}{A_V} = \frac{k_{\lambda}}{R_V}\]

is taken from a table of absorption from 0.1 to 10 \(\mu m\) in the bands U, B, V and I. The absorption \(A_{\lambda}\) is normalized to \(A_V = 1.0\).

Interpolation is required for wavelengths between those in the table. Extrapolation for longer and shorter wavelengths in not implemented at this time.

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Wavelength array in Angstroms
  • flux (float) – Flux array
  • ebv (float) – Color excess, E(B-V).
  • RV (Optional[float]) – Ratio of total selection extinction. Defaults to 3.1
Returns:

reddenedflux – Spectrum reddened according to the Allen law.
Return type:

float
calz(wave, flux, ebv, RV=4.05)¶

Applies the reddening law for starburst galaxies from Calzetti et al. (2000).

The Calzetti law is valid for galaxy spectra where massive stars dominate the radiation output and between 0.12 and 2.2 \(\mu m\). calz() is based on the IDL procedure CALZ_UNRED() written by W. Landsman, which extrapolates between 0.12 and 0.0912 \(\mu m\). Here, the wavelength range is extended below 0.0912.

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Wavelength array in Angstroms
  • flux (float) – Flux array
  • ebv (float) – Color excess, E(B-V). Should be that derived for the stellar continuum, \(E(B-V)_{stars}\), which is related to the reddening derived from the gas, \(E(B-V)_{gas}\), via the Balmer decrement: \(E(B-V)_{stars} = 0.44 * E(B-V)_{gas}\).
  • RV (Optional[float]) – Ratio of total selection extinction. Defaults to 4.05
Returns:

reddenedflux – Spectrum reddened according to the Calzetti law.
Return type:

float
ccm(wave, tauV, RV=3.1)¶

Applies the reddening law from Cardelli, Clayton, and Mathis (1989).

The reddening law includes the update for the near-UV given by O’Donnell (1994). ccm() is based on the IDL procedure CCM_UNRED() written by W. Landsman. Parameterization is valid from the far-UV to the IR (0.1 to 3.5 \(\mu m\)).

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Wavelength array in Angstroms
  • flux (float) – Flux array
  • ebv (float) – Color excess, E(B-V).
  • RV (Optional[float]) – Ratio of total selection extinction. Defaults to 3.1
Returns:

reddenedflux – Spectrum reddened according to the CCM law.
Return type:

float

fitz(wave, flux, ebv, RV=3.1)¶

Applies the reddening law for the LMC from Fitzpatrick (1986).

The reddening law is published as:

\[\frac{E(\lambda - V)}{E(B-V)} = (\frac{A_{\lambda}}{A_V} - 1)*R_V,\]

and is valid for \(x < 8.3 \mu m^{-1}\) (\(\lambda > 0.12 \mu m\)). Extrapolation at shorter wavelengths is not implemented at this time. At wavelengths longer than \(0.333 \mu m\), the points from allen() are adopted to avoid \(k_{\lambda}\) being too flat in the red and near-IR regions.

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Wavelength array in Angstroms
  • flux (float) – Flux array
  • ebv (float) – Color excess, E(B-V).
  • RV (Optional[float]) – Ratio of total selection extinction. Defaults to 3.1
Returns:

reddenedflux – Spectrum reddened according to the Fitzpatrick law.
Return type:

float

prevot(wave, flux, ebv, RV=2.72)¶

Applies the reddening law for the SMC from Prevot et al. (1984).

The reddening law is published as:

\[\frac{E(\lambda - V)}{E(B-V)} = (\frac{A_{\lambda}}{A_V} - 1)*R_V,\]

and is valid from 0.1275 - 0.32 \(\mu m\) (B and V). Values for \(\lambda > 0.32\mu m\) (for U, J, H and K) are taken from Bouchet et al. (1985).

Interpolation is required for wavelengths in between those in Prevot’s table. Extrapolation is needed at shorter wavelengths to cover Ly \(\alpha\).

Parameters:
  • wave (float) – Wavelength array in Angstroms
  • flux (float) – Flux array
  • ebv (float) – Color excess, E(B-V).
  • RV (Optional[float]) – Ratio of total selection extinction. Defaults to 2.72
Returns:

reddenedflux – Spectrum reddened according to the Prevot law.
Return type:

float
seaton(wave, flux, ebv, RV=3.1)¶

Applies the reddening law for the MW from Seaton (1979)

The reddening law is published as:

\[\frac{E(\lambda - V)}{E(B-V)} = (\frac{A_{\lambda}}{A_V} - 1)*R_V,\]

and is valid for \(x < 8.3 \mu m^{-1}\) (\(\lambda > 0.12 \mu m\)). Extrapolation at shorter wavelengths is not implemented at this time. At wavelengths longer than \(0.365 \mu m\), the points from allen() are adopted to avoid \(k_{\lambda}\) being too flat in the red and near-IR regions. The reddening law used here is as fit by Fitzpatrick (1986)
